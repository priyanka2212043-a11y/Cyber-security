Save Our Oceans - Detailed Information

üö® Crisis Level: URGENT

8 million tons of plastic enter our oceans every year. Marine life is dying, ecosystems are collapsing, and microplastics are now in our food chain.

The Problem

  • Plastic Pollution: 8 million tons of plastic waste enters oceans annually, forming massive garbage patches
  • Marine Death: Over 100,000 marine animals die each year from plastic entanglement and ingestion
  • Coral Destruction: 50% of coral reefs have died in the last 30 years due to pollution and climate change
  • Overfishing: 90% of large fish populations have been depleted since 1950
  • Industrial Pollution: Chemical runoff from factories and agriculture creating dead zones

Our Demands

  • Immediate ban on single-use plastics in all member countries
  • Hold top 20 plastic-producing corporations financially responsible for ocean cleanup
  • Establish marine protected areas covering 30% of global oceans by 2025
  • Enforce strict regulations on industrial ocean dumping and chemical runoff
  • $50 billion annual fund for ocean restoration and cleanup projects

‚ö†Ô∏è Crisis Level: ACTIVE

We lose 10 million hectares of forest every year - equivalent to 27 soccer fields every minute. Ancient forests that took millennia to grow are being destroyed for short-term profit.

The Problem

  • Illegal Logging: Estimated 30% of global timber trade is illegal, worth $152 billion annually
  • Deforestation Rate: 10 million hectares lost per year (size of Iceland)
  • Biodiversity Loss: Forests house 80% of terrestrial species - all at risk
  • Climate Impact: Deforestation causes 10% of global greenhouse emissions
  • Indigenous Rights: Forest destruction displaces indigenous communities who've protected these lands for generations

Our Demands

  • End all illegal logging with strict enforcement and criminal penalties
  • Zero deforestation commitments from all major corporations by 2024
  • Recognize and protect indigenous land rights and forest guardianship
  • Restore 350 million hectares of degraded forest land by 2030
  • Ban imports from companies linked to deforestation

100% Renewable Energy by 2030 - Detailed Information

‚úÖ Status: ACHIEVABLE

The technology exists. Solar and wind are now cheaper than fossil fuels. The only barrier is political will and corporate resistance.

The Problem

  • Fossil Fuel Dependence: 84% of global energy still comes from coal, oil, and gas
  • Climate Crisis: Energy sector produces 73% of global greenhouse gas emissions
  • Corporate Resistance: Fossil fuel industry spends $200 million annually lobbying against clean energy
  • Subsidies: Governments give $7 trillion to fossil fuels while renewable energy gets fraction of support
  • Job Myth: Renewable energy creates 3x more jobs than fossil fuels per dollar invested

Our Demands

  • 100% renewable energy commitment from G20 countries by 2030
  • End all fossil fuel subsidies immediately and redirect to renewable infrastructure
  • No new fossil fuel projects - no new coal plants, oil wells, or gas pipelines
  • Just transition guarantees - renewable energy jobs and retraining for fossil fuel workers
  • $500 billion annual investment in renewable energy grid infrastructure

Campaign Progress

Signatures Collected: 850,000 / 3 million goal

Governments Targeted: G20 countries, major energy corporations

Cities Committed: 180 cities pledged 100% renewable by 2030

Progress: 28% toward goal

Why 2030?

Scientists warn we have until 2030 to cut emissions by 45% to avoid catastrophic climate change. Transitioning to renewable energy is the fastest way to achieve this. Every year of delay makes recovery harder and more expensive.
